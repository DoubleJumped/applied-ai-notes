The journey

From messy workflow to trusted production system.

The forward deployed engineering playbook, told the way change actually happens: what is — and what could be.

What is

Everyone owns the same intelligence.

Every frontier model, on tap, for anyone who pays. Fifty enterprises, one identical stack. Whatever advantage intelligence conferred is already gone.

What could be

The edge moves to deployment.

Where, how, and why intelligence is applied to this business, with its specific processes, systems, and exceptions — that is defensible.

What is

95% of AI pilots fail.

Token maxing: give everything to the model and hope. Ten-million-dollar budgets burned in a quarter without moving the needle.

What could be

One person bridges business and machine: the FDE.

The best combination of consultant and engineer — not the average. Business reality → judgment → deployed system.

What is

The documented process is fiction.

"An email arrives" — from 40 senders, no two alike. Four spreadsheets, one quietly wrong. An internal system with no API, and the routing rules living in one person's head.

What could be

Discovery that maps reality.

Shadow the work for full days. Read the real artifacts. Sit with every owner of the process. The exceptions become the spec.

What is

AI slapped everywhere, understood nowhere.

Every workflow gets a model whether it needs one or not. Risky steps automated, trivial steps over-engineered, ROI never asked.

What could be

An audit that ends in one operating map.

Current state, future state, one use case chosen for value, explicit boundaries — and the expected value quantified in revenue, risk, and cost.

What is

Happy-path demos.

Impressive on stage, dead in production. One way to go right gets built; the thousand ways to go wrong get discovered by the client.

What could be

Systems built for the unhappy paths.

Guardrails, structured outputs, checkpoints, resume-after-failure, retries with backoff — and an audit trail of every step, because no traces means no trust.

What is

"It seems to work."

Confidence by anecdote. Nobody can say what the system gets wrong, how often, or what happens when it does.

What could be

Evidence: a graded golden dataset.

41 of 50 runs pass — and the nine failures are categorized, investigated, and fed back. Below 0.80 confidence, escalate. High-risk actions always reach a person.

What is

"First, migrate off your ERP."

The pitch that kills the project. Years and millions invested in the current stack, and the AI vendor's opening move is to throw it away.

What could be

Integrate, sandbox, earn autonomy.

Build on top of what exists. Test inside their walls. Shadow mode, then human approval, then autonomy — each step earned by evidence.

What is

To your sponsor, you are a risk.

The status quo is safe. Bringing you in is not — if it fails, it's their name attached. Fear, not technology, is what stalls adoption.

What could be

Your job is to get them promoted.

Visible traces, approval gates, measurable checkpoints — then attributable value in the three buckets, delivered face to face. The relationship is infrastructure.

New bliss

The loop runs again — and you own the path.

Audit → build → evals → deploy → observe → improve. Each stage earns the right to the next. One workflow becomes the whole business.
